Shawerma

Shawerma is a popular Middle Eastern dish made of marinated meat that is cooked on a vertical rotisserie and served in a wrap or pita with various toppings.

Ingredients

  • Chicken or lamb (thinly sliced)
  • Yogurt
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Lemon juice
  • Olive oil
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Paprika
  • Salt and pepper
  • saj bread
  • Tomatoes (sliced)
  • Cucumber (sliced)
  • Pickles
  • Tahini sauce or garlic sauce
  • Fresh parsley (chopped)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, minced garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, cumin, coriander, paprika, salt, and pepper to create a marinade.
  2. Add the thinly sliced meat to the marinade and let it sit for at least 1 hour (or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor).
  3. Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the marinated meat until fully cooked and slightly charred.
  4. Warm the pita bread or flatbreads on the grill or in a pan.
  5. Assemble the shawerma by placing the cooked meat on the bread, then adding sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, pickles, and a drizzle of tahini sauce or garlic sauce.
  6. Sprinkle with fresh parsley before wrapping it up.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade shawerma!

Notes

You can customize the toppings based on your preferences. Some people like to add onions, lettuce, or even fries inside the wrap.

Serving Suggestions

Shawerma is often served with a side of fries, salad, or rice. It can also be accompanied by pickled vegetables for added flavor.

Storage

Leftover shawerma can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in a skillet or microwave before serving.

Variations

Try using different meats like beef or turkey, or make a vegetarian version with grilled vegetables or falafel.

History

Shawerma has its roots in the Ottoman Empire and is believed to have originated in Turkey. It has since become a beloved street food across the Middle East and beyond, with each region adding its own twist to the recipe.

Fun Fact

The word "shawerma" comes from the Arabic word "shawarma," which means "turning" or "rotating," referring to the way the meat is cooked on a vertical spit.
