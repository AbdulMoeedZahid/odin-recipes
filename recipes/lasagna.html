Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta, cheese, and meat sauce.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Marinara sauce
  • Eggs
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  3. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
  4. Add ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
  5. Stir in marinara sauce, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, and a pinch of salt.
  7. Spread a layer of meat sauce in a baking dish, followed by a layer of noodles, then a layer of the ricotta mixture, and a layer of mozzarella cheese.
  8. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes until bubbly and golden brown.
  10. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Notes

Feel free to customize the recipe by adding vegetables like spinach or mushrooms, or using different types of cheese.

Serving Suggestions

Serve with a side salad and garlic bread for a complete meal.

Storage

Lasagna can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 3 months. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Variations

Try using different meats like sausage or turkey, or make a vegetarian version with layers of roasted vegetables.

History

Lasagna has its origins in ancient Greece and Rome, where layers of pasta were used in various dishes. The modern version we know today evolved in Italy, particularly in the region of Emilia-Romagna.
