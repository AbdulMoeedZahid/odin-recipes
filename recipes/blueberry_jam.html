Blueberry Jam

Blueberry jam is a sweet spread made from blueberries, sugar, and pectin, perfect for breakfast or as a topping for desserts.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon pectin (optional, for thicker jam)

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the blueberries, sugar, lemon juice, and water.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring gently until the sugar dissolves and the blueberries start to release their juices.
  3. If using pectin, stir it in at this point and bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the mixture thickens to your desired consistency.
  5. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before transferring to sterilized jars.
  6. Seal the jars and let them cool completely before storing in the refrigerator.
  7. Enjoy your homemade blueberry jam on toast, pancakes, or as a topping for desserts!

Notes

Make sure to use ripe blueberries for the best flavor. You can adjust the sugar based on your taste preference.

Serving Suggestions

Blueberry jam is delicious on toast, bagels, or pancakes. It can also be used as a filling for pastries or as a topping for yogurt and ice cream.

Storage

Store the blueberry jam in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. For longer storage, consider canning it using proper canning techniques.

Variations

You can add spices like cinnamon or nutmeg for extra flavor, or mix in other fruits like raspberries or strawberries for a mixed berry jam.

History

Blueberry jam has been a popular preserve in North America, especially in regions where blueberries are abundant. It is often associated with summer harvests and is a staple in many households.
